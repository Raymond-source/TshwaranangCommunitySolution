Who We Are

Tshwaranang Community Solution is a Non-Profit Organisation registered under NPO Act 71 of 1997 (Registration Number 333-527-NPO). It is also registered as a Non Profit Company under Companies Act 71 of 2008 (Registration Number 2026/121811/08), as well as a Public Benefit Organisation under Income Tax Act 58 of 1962 (Registration Number 930088793).

We are registered with National Treasurer Central Database Supplier: MAAA1697303.

Our Story

Tshwaranang Community was founded by Mr Xolani Nkosi and Sr Phumi Phogole, who saw a need to provide extended HIV Prevention, Treatment and Care targeting Taxi Operators, Street Vendors and General Commuters around 5 Major Taxi Ranks in Tshwane.

Currently we have a verbal MoU with Gauteng SANTACO PEC and Department of Transport. We also maintain a technical relationship with Wits RHI Gophelega Project under Dr Josephine Ochere-Darko.

Our Mission

To provide accessible High quality HIV and Wellness services directly to Taxi Operators, Street Vendors and daily commuters through Fixed Walk-In Health Hubs at major Taxi Ranks.

Our Vision

To transform the Taxi Industry into a powerful engine for Health and Climate Change Resilience by 2028, ensuring all major Taxi Ranks in Tshwane are hubs for accessible care.

Our Goals

What we aim to achieve

Mission Goals

  • Provide accessible High quality HIV and Wellness directly to Taxi Operators, Street Vendors and daily commuters via Fixed Walk-In Health Hubs at Marabastad, Bosman, Bloedmall, Mabopane Interchange and Donneboom Taxi Ranks
  • Distribute trees for household yards through 1 Million Tree Campaign to improve air pollution within Major Taxi Ranks in Tshwane
  • Revive door-size Vegetable Gardens to improve Food Security
  • Achieve this through strategic partnership (NaTIS, DEKRA & DTI) and Stigma-free Health Education

Vision Goals

  • Clean and Safe Water banking through water banking initiatives (Borehole and Jojo Tanks)
  • Cooling areas during Hot season through our Tree Project
  • Empowering workforce and commuters
  • HIV Prevention, Treatment and accessible care for all
